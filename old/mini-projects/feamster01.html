Problem

The Internet is composed of independently operated networks called Autonomous Systems, or "ASes" (Georgia Tech is an example of an AS; your home broadband provider is another). The conenctions relationships between these ASes collectively form a network we know as the Internet. Studying how this AS-level graph evolves can provide insight into why a network exhibits a certain structure.

Previous work has observed that the Internet's AS-level graph exhibits "power law" characteristics. Subsequent work mildly debunked this by suggesting that the power-law characteristics were artifacts of measurement, and that, in any case, describing a topology as "power law" is not evocative: it doesn't describe why a graph exhibits a certain structure in the first place. In this project, you will study the evolution of the AS graph over time. Where are new links forming and old links disappearing? Does the appearance or disappearance of these links suggest anything about how relationships on the Internet are changing (e.g., are there more links between ASes at the edge of the graph)? Is there a rhyme or reason to how links appear and disappear? Can you model it somehow?
