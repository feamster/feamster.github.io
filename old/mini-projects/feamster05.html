Problem

While all of us are victims of spam (e.g., unsolicited commercial email), little is known about the processes and infrastructure that spammers use.

For example, it is widely believed that spammers use large groups of compromised hosts ("botnets") from which to send spam. How large are these botnets, and how many distinct botnets can you detect? Do different sets of spammers share this infrastructure? Others have claimed (and I have some preliminary evidence to suggest) that some spammers temporarily steal ("hijack") IP address space from which to send spam. What other techniques can you detect? Can you apply your findings to automatically detect spam techniques in real time?

Background

The popular press has quite a few reports of spamming techniques.

  • MailAvenger: the mail server that is running the spam "honeypot" at MIT. The server is configurable: for example, we have configured it to run traceroutes to the mail relays, perform passive OS fingerprinting and DNS blacklist lookups, etc. at the time the spam is received. We can extend it to collect other data that might be helpful in your study.
  • SpamHaus: an organization that keeps track of spammers.
  • Coordinated Spamming
  • Spam Statistics at MIT
You may also find SpamKings to be an interesting read.

Deliverables

A short (less than 10-page) writeup describing your findings. You may also choose to incorporate your findings into a spam detection algorithm (e.g., MailAvenger, SpamAssassin).

Evaluation

A report describing your findings is sufficient. Incorporating your techniques into existing spam detection software and demonstrating that your techniques could be effective in detecting spam is even better.
