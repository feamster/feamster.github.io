Scheduling Meetings

Your process for scheduling a meeting with me
  1. If you want to schedule a meeting and you are not scheduling for a group, use Calendly to request a meeting. A meeting agenda is required. The meeting slots offered are by design, and meeting slots only are available with ample notice, so there's no reason to send me an email asking me for a meeting. You can assume that if a slot is being offered there, I'm free and willing to meet.
  2. If you are a UChicago or other collaborator, and the above does not apply to you (e.g., if you're scheduling for a group), you can send a meeting request to my UChicago email address. Make sure to send to @uchicago.edu or the request won't show up on my calendar. This scheduling mode likely implies that we already have an existing email thread or working relationship, as well. If we don't, return to step 1. As you may gather from looking at available slots in Step 1, I strongly prefer afternoon meetings, so please default to those times if possible.
  3. If all else fails, send an email to Katie Rosengarten at UChicago with subject line "Nick Feamster Meeting Request", and she can help us find a time.
